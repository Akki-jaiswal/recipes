Quick Vegetable Stir Fry

Description

This quick and easy vegetable stir fry recipe is perfect for a healthy weeknight meal. It features a colorful medley of fresh vegetables cooked quickly in a hot wok or skillet, maintaining their crisp texture and vibrant flavors. Tossed in a savory, light sauce, this dish is highly customizable based on your favorite vegetables and can be served over rice or noodles.

Stir-frying is a fantastic way to prepare vegetables as it preserves their nutrients and natural goodness. This recipe emphasizes fresh ingredients and a balanced sauce to create a delicious and satisfying dish in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil (or sesame oil for added flavor)
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 2 carrots, thinly sliced or julienned
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas or snow peas
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms (e.g., shiitake or cremini)
  • 1/2 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • **For the Stir Fry Sauce:**
    • 1/4 cup soy sauce (low sodium preferred)
    • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
    • 1 tbsp brown sugar or honey
    • 1 tsp cornstarch (mixed with 1 tbsp water to make a slurry)
    • 1/2 tsp sesame oil (optional)

Steps

  1. **Prepare the Sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar/honey, cornstarch slurry, and optional sesame oil. Set aside.
  2. **Heat Wok/Skillet:** Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or a large skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  3. **Cook Harder Vegetables:** Add broccoli florets and sliced carrots to the hot wok. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until slightly tender-crisp.
  4. **Add Softer Vegetables & Aromatics:** Add red bell pepper, snap peas, mushrooms, sliced onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until vegetables are bright and tender-crisp. Avoid overcooking.
  5. **Add Sauce:** Give the prepared sauce a quick whisk again (as cornstarch can settle) and pour it over the vegetables in the wok.
  6. **Thicken Sauce:** Cook, stirring constantly, for 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats the vegetables.
  7. **Serve:** Remove from heat immediately. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds or chopped green onions if desired.
